# Research Questions and Hypotheses

## Overview

This document defines the main scientific questions, research objectives, and hypotheses investigated in the TennisLabAI project.

The project focuses on multimodal tennis biomechanics analysis by combining wearable inertial sensors, markerless computer vision, musculoskeletal simulation, and artificial intelligence.

---

# Main Research Question

## RQ1

How accurately can markerless computer vision estimate tennis biomechanical variables compared with laboratory-grade wearable inertial sensing systems?

---

# Secondary Research Questions

## RQ2

What is the level of agreement between camera-based motion estimation and IMU-based measurements for tennis movements?

---

## RQ3

Which biomechanical variables can be reliably estimated using markerless motion capture?

Examples:

- Joint angles
- Range of motion
- Segment orientation
- Movement velocity

---

## RQ4

Can multimodal fusion of computer vision and IMU data improve biomechanical estimation accuracy and robustness?

---

## RQ5

How can musculoskeletal simulation improve the interpretation of movement patterns extracted from sensor and vision data?

---

## RQ6

Can artificial intelligence models identify performance-related movement patterns and support tennis technique analysis?

---

# Research Hypotheses

## H1: Markerless Motion Estimation

Markerless computer vision can estimate selected biomechanical variables with acceptable agreement compared with wearable inertial sensing.

---

## H2: Multimodal Sensor Fusion

Combining visual and inertial information improves robustness and reduces estimation uncertainty compared with using a single sensing modality.

---

## H3: Biomechanical Feature Learning

Biomechanical features extracted from multimodal data can provide meaningful information for tennis performance assessment.

---

## H4: Simulation-Based Interpretation

Musculoskeletal simulation provides additional biomechanical insights that cannot be obtained directly from camera or IMU measurements.

---

# Expected Scientific Contributions

The project aims to contribute:

- A reproducible multimodal framework for tennis biomechanics.
- A systematic comparison between IMU-based and vision-based motion analysis.
- Methods for sensor fusion in sports biomechanics.
- AI-based approaches for movement understanding.

---

# Evaluation Criteria

The proposed methods will be evaluated using:

- Root Mean Square Error (RMSE)
- Mean Absolute Error (MAE)
- Correlation analysis
- Intraclass Correlation Coefficient (ICC)
- Bland-Altman analysis
